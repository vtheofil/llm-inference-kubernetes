FRANCE TRAVEL GUIDE
===================

France is the world's most visited country, drawing nearly 90 million tourists annually. From the City of Light to the French Riviera, Normandy's beaches to the châteaux of the Loire Valley, France offers extraordinary diversity.

MAIN CITIES
-----------

PARIS
The capital and cultural heart of France, Paris is one of the world's great cities — a global center of art, fashion, gastronomy, and culture.
- Population: ~2.2 million (metro: ~12 million)
- Best for: Art, architecture, cuisine, romance, shopping, history
- Airports: Charles de Gaulle (CDG) — main hub; Orly (ORY) — south; Beauvais (BVA) — Ryanair/budget
- Getting around: Metro (16 lines), RER trains, buses, Vélib' bike share, walking

Key attractions in Paris:
- Eiffel Tower: €26.80 (summit, adult) / €17.10 (2nd floor, adult); stairs to 2nd floor €11.50; book online (essential in summer)
- Louvre Museum: €22; free under 18 and for EU residents under 26; closed Tuesdays; book online
- Musée d'Orsay: €16; impressionist masterpieces (Monet, Van Gogh, Renoir); closed Mondays
- Centre Pompidou: €15 (permanent collection + temporary); free first Sunday of month
- Musée de l'Orangerie (Monet's Water Lilies): €12.50; closed Tuesdays
- Notre-Dame Cathedral: FREE (currently under restoration after 2019 fire; exterior viewing)
- Sacré-Cœur Basilica: FREE (Montmartre hilltop; funicular €2.15 each way or 185 steps)
- Arc de Triomphe: €13 (roof access and museum); view from Champs-Élysées: FREE
- Palace of Versailles: €21 (palace) + €10 (gardens on fountain show days); 40 min by RER C; very crowded in summer
- Musée Rodin: €13 (home of The Thinker); beautiful garden
- Sainte-Chapelle: €13 (stunning Gothic stained glass)
- Catacombs of Paris: €29 (book online; limited capacity)
- Musée Picasso: €14; closed Mondays
- Père Lachaise Cemetery: FREE (Jim Morrison, Édith Piaf, Oscar Wilde buried here)
- Paris Sewer Museum (Musée des Égouts): €8

Paris Museum Pass: €52 (2 days), €66 (4 days), €78 (6 days) — covers 50+ museums including Louvre, Orsay, Versailles, Pompidou.

NICE AND THE FRENCH RIVIERA
Nice is the capital of the Côte d'Azur, perfect for beach holidays combined with culture.
- Population: ~340,000
- Best for: Mediterranean beaches, art, day trips to Monaco, Cannes, Antibes
- Airport: Nice Côte d'Azur (NCE)
- Getting around: Tram, buses, TER trains along the coast

Key attractions:
- Promenade des Anglais: FREE (5km beachfront walk)
- Old Town (Vieux-Nice): FREE to wander; colorful baroque architecture
- Colline du Château (Castle Hill): FREE (panoramic views; elevator €2 or free hike)
- Musée Matisse: FREE (Nice's most famous museum)
- Musée d'Art Moderne et Contemporain (MAMAC): FREE
- Monaco day trip: 25 min by train (€4 one-way); Monte Carlo Casino (€17 entry), Prince's Palace (€10), Oceanographic Museum (€19)
- Cannes: 35 min by TER train (€7); walk La Croisette, visit Îles de Lérins (€16 return ferry)
- Antibes: 25 min by train (€5); Picasso Museum (€8), ramparts, Port Vauban

LYON
France's culinary capital and second most important city historically.
- Population: ~520,000
- Best for: Food (bouchons lyonnais), silk history, old town (UNESCO), day trips to Beaujolais/Burgundy
- Getting around: Metro, tram, funicular
- Key sites: Vieux Lyon (UNESCO, FREE to walk), Basilique de Fourvière (FREE), Musée des Confluences (€9), Musée des Beaux-Arts (€8), Institut Lumière (€8, birthplace of cinema)
- Bouchon lunch (traditional Lyonnais restaurant): €18–25 for full meal

BORDEAUX
World's wine capital, recently revitalized and voted best European destination.
- Population: ~260,000
- Best for: Wine tourism, architecture, food, Atlantic coast
- Key sites: Place de la Bourse + Water Mirror (FREE), La Cité du Vin wine museum (€22), Darwin Ecosystem (FREE creative quarter)
- Wine tasting at châteaux: €15–60 per tasting; book ahead

STRASBOURG
Charming Alsatian city blending French and German cultures.
- Best for: Christmas market (best in France), European Parliament, cathedral, Germanic half-timbered houses
- Key sites: Notre-Dame de Strasbourg Cathedral (€6 for towers), Petite France quarter (FREE), European Parliament (FREE tours Mon–Fri)

TRANSPORTATION
--------------

Getting to France:
- Flights Athens to Paris: €80–220 return (Aegean, Air France, Transavia, Ryanair via secondary airports)
- Budget airlines to Nice: Ryanair, easyJet from €50–150 return

Within France by train (SNCF / TGV):
- Paris to Lyon: ~2h, €30–70 (TGV; book early for €19–29 promo fares)
- Paris to Nice: ~5.5h, €40–100 (TGV+TER; or fly in 1.5h for similar price)
- Paris to Bordeaux: ~2h, €30–80 (TGV)
- Paris to Strasbourg: ~1.75h, €25–70 (TGV)
- Lyon to Bordeaux: ~2h by TGV

Budget tip: "Ouigo" is SNCF's low-cost TGV service with tickets from €10–29 if booked far ahead (from suburban stations, less convenient).

Navigo Paris metro pass: €30/week (zones 1–5, includes Versailles and CDG airport) — excellent value for 5+ days in Paris.

Local Paris transport:
- Single metro ticket (t+): €2.15
- Book of 10 (carnet): €17.35 (via app: "Bonjour RATP")
- Day pass (Mobilis Zone 1–2): €8.65
- Airport bus (Orly): €12 / CDG Bus: €12

FOOD AND DINING
---------------

France is legendary for its cuisine. Even budget travelers eat well.

Typical costs:
- Café coffee (espresso): €2–3.50
- Croissant/pain au chocolat: €1.50–2.50
- Baguette sandwich (jambon-beurre): €4–6
- Crêpe from street vendor: €3–6
- Lunch formule (entrée + plat or plat + dessert): €13–18 (excellent value, especially in Lyon)
- Pizza/pasta restaurant: €12–20 for main
- Brasserie/bistro main course: €16–28
- Gastronomic restaurant (full menu): €50–200+
- Glass of wine at bar: €4–8
- Beer (demi, 250ml): €4–6
- Picnic from supermarket: €8–12 (cheese, baguette, wine)

Paris food tips:
- Street food: falafel in Marais (L'As du Falafel €7.50), crêpes in Montmartre, macarons at Ladurée (~€3 each)
- Lunch is always cheaper than dinner at the same restaurant
- "Formule déjeuner" (lunch menu) offers best value

BUDGET PLANNING
---------------

3-DAY PARIS ITINERARY

Budget approach (~€400–500 total / Mid-range ~€650–850 / Luxury €1,200+)

Day 1 — Iconic Paris:
- Eiffel Tower (€17–27): go at opening time to avoid queues
- Trocadéro Gardens: FREE (perfect photo spot)
- Champ de Mars picnic lunch: €10–12 (supermarket)
- Musée d'Orsay (€16): book online, closed Monday
- Evening: Pont des Arts + Notre-Dame exterior + Île de la Cité walk
- Dinner: Bistro near Latin Quarter €20–30

Day 2 — Art and History:
- Louvre Museum (€22): arrive at opening; go Wednesday or Friday (open until 21:45)
- Lunch: Cafe inside Louvre courtyard or nearby bakery €8–12
- Palais Royal gardens: FREE
- Marais neighborhood walk: FREE (Place des Vosges, Jewish Quarter, trendy shops)
- Musée Picasso or Centre Pompidou (€14–15)
- Evening: Apéro at wine bar in Marais €10–15

Day 3 — Versailles or Montmartre:
Option A: Versailles (€21 + €17 RER train round trip = €38): full day
Option B: Montmartre (FREE): Sacré-Cœur, artist quarter, viewpoints; afternoon: Moulin Rouge area, flea market at Clignancourt
- Dinner: Traditional bistro €25–35

3-day Paris budget breakdown:
- Budget: Hostel €35–45/night × 3 = €105–135 + Metro €25 + Activities €50–60 + Food €100–140 = TOTAL ~€280–360
- Mid-range: Hotel €160/night × 3 = €480 + Metro/Uber €40 + Activities €90 + Food €180–220 = TOTAL ~€790–830
- Luxury: Hotel €350/night × 3 = €1,050 + Activities €150+ + Food €300+ = TOTAL ~€1,500+

Note: Paris Museum Pass (4-day €66) is worth it if visiting 3+ museums.

5-DAY ITINERARY — PARIS + NICE

Day 1–3: Paris (see above)
Day 4: Train or fly to Nice (~5.5h TGV €50–90, or fly €50–80)
  - Afternoon: Promenade des Anglais walk, Old Town, beach
  - Dinner: Socca (chickpea pancake, €3–4), pan bagnat (local sandwich €5–7), seafood restaurant €25–40
Day 5: Nice — Matisse Museum (FREE) + Castle Hill (FREE) + Monaco day trip (€4 train + €17 casino entry)

5-day budget summary:
- Budget: ~€550–700 (hostels, budget train, self-catering some meals)
- Mid-range: ~€1,000–1,300 (3-star hotels, restaurant meals, museum pass)
- Luxury: ~€2,000–3,000+

7-DAY ITINERARY — PARIS + LOIRE VALLEY + BORDEAUX

Day 1–3: Paris
Day 4: Loire Valley day trip or 1-night stay (châteaux of Chambord €14, Chenonceau €15.50, Amboise €15); rent bikes €15/day
Day 5: Travel to Bordeaux (TGV ~2h from Paris; from Loire ~3.5h); evening wine tasting
Day 6: Bordeaux — Cité du Vin (€22), old town, wine district Saint-Émilion (day trip, bus €6 or tour €30–50)
Day 7: Return to Paris or fly home from Bordeaux

7-day budget summary:
- Budget: ~€800–1,000 (hostels + cheapest SNCF tickets booked early)
- Mid-range: ~€1,400–1,800
- Luxury: ~€3,000–5,000+

MONEY SAVING TIPS FOR FRANCE
------------------------------
1. Navigo weekly pass: best deal in Paris if staying 5+ days (€30 covers all zones including airport)
2. Museum Pass saves money if visiting 3+ museums in Paris
3. Lunch formule: always order the set lunch menu (€13–18) over à la carte
4. Picnic culture: French people picnic in parks; supermarket + wine + baguette = €10–15 for two
5. Free museums first Sunday: Louvre, Orsay, Pompidou free first Sunday of month (very crowded)
6. OUIGO trains: SNCF budget train from €10 if booked months ahead
7. Vélib' bike share in Paris: €5 for 24h access (first 30 min each ride free)
8. Many churches are FREE: Sainte-Chapelle (€13 worth it), but Sacré-Cœur, Notre-Dame, etc. are free
9. BlaBlaCar carpooling: Paris to Lyon for €15–25 vs train €50–70
10. Duty-free shopping at CDG if EU resident: save 15–20% on luxury goods

BEST TIME TO VISIT FRANCE
--------------------------
- Spring (April–June): BEST — mild weather, Paris in bloom, before peak crowds
- Summer (July–Aug): Riviera peak season (busy, pricey); Paris slightly emptier in August as locals leave
- Autumn (Sept–Oct): Excellent — harvest in wine regions, autumn colors, good weather in south
- Winter (Dec): Christmas markets in Alsace (Strasbourg #1 in Europe); Paris magical but cold
