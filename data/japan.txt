MONEY SAVING TIPS FOR JAPAN
-----------------------------
1. Convenience stores (7-Eleven, Lawson, FamilyMart): best cheap food; ¬•150 onigiri, ¬•500 bento, great coffee
2. JR Pass: buy before you leave home (must be ordered abroad); calculate if your itinerary justifies the cost
3. Free temples: many shrine grounds (Meiji Jingu, Fushimi Inari) are free; only buildings cost money
4. IC Card (Suica): load it and tap everywhere ‚ÄĒ works on all trains, buses, in convenience stores
5. Go to Fushimi Inari at dawn (06:00): most iconic photo, zero crowd, completely free
6. Skip taxis: Japan's train/subway system is world-class and covers everywhere
7. Gyudon chains (Yoshinoya, Sukiya): full hot meal for ¬•400‚Äď600 (~‚ā¨2.50‚Äď3.75)
8. Stand-up udon/soba: ¬•400‚Äď700 lunch, faster than sit-down, equally authentic
9. Osaka is significantly cheaper than Tokyo for accommodation
10. Day trips by local train (Kamakura, Nara, Nikko) are much cheaper than Shinkansen
11. Book teamLab and popular experiences online ‚ÄĒ they sell out weeks ahead
12. Yen is historically weak (2024): very favorable exchange rate for European tourists

BEST TIME TO VISIT JAPAN
--------------------------
- Spring (late March‚ÄďApril): BEST ‚ÄĒ cherry blossoms (sakura); book accommodation 6+ months ahead as it sells out
- Autumn (mid-October‚ÄďNovember): BEST ‚ÄĒ autumn foliage (koyo), mild temperatures, fewer crowds than spring
- Summer (June‚ÄďAugust): Hot and humid; tsuyu (rainy season) in June; expensive domestic travel (Obon holiday Aug 13‚Äď16)
- Winter (December‚ÄďFebruary): Cold but atmospheric; fewer tourists; ski resorts in Hokkaido/Nagano; onsen season; best time for budget travel
- Golden Week (April 29‚ÄďMay 5): AVOID ‚ÄĒ all Japanese travel simultaneously; prices double, everything booked out
