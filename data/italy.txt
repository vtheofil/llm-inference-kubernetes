ITALY TRAVEL GUIDE
==================

Italy is one of Europe's most beloved destinations, offering world-class art, history, cuisine, and natural beauty. From the ruins of Rome to the canals of Venice and the rolling hills of Tuscany, Italy has something for every traveler.

MAIN CITIES
-----------

ROME (Roma)
Rome is the eternal city, capital of Italy and home to nearly 3,000 years of history. It sits in central Italy and serves as the main entry point for most visitors.
- Population: ~2.8 million
- Best for: History, art, religion, food
- Airport: Leonardo da Vinci (Fiumicino, FCO) — 30km from center; Ciampino (CIA) for low-cost airlines
- Getting around: Metro (lines A, B, C), buses, taxis, walking

Key attractions in Rome:
- Colosseum + Roman Forum + Palatine Hill: €18 combined ticket (book in advance to skip queues; fast-track tickets ~€24)
- Vatican Museums + Sistine Chapel: €20 (online), €17 (on-site, with long queues); book 2–3 weeks ahead in high season
- St. Peter's Basilica: FREE, but climb the dome €8 (stairs) or €10 (lift)
- Trevi Fountain: FREE (open 24h; go early morning to avoid crowds)
- Pantheon: €5 (as of 2023)
- Borghese Gallery: €15 (reservation mandatory, max 2h per group)
- Piazza Navona: FREE
- Spanish Steps: FREE
- Campo de' Fiori market: FREE (mornings)
- Castel Sant'Angelo: €15

FLORENCE (Firenze)
The cradle of the Renaissance, Florence is compact and walkable, home to the greatest concentration of art in the world.
- Population: ~370,000
- Best for: Art (Renaissance), architecture, Tuscan food, day trips to Tuscany
- Airport: Amerigo Vespucci (FLR) — small; many fly into Pisa (PSA, 80km, 1h by train)
- Getting around: Mostly on foot; buses available

Key attractions in Florence:
- Uffizi Gallery: €25 (mandatory online booking; closed Mondays); home to Botticelli's Birth of Venus
- Accademia Gallery (Michelangelo's David): €16 (book online); expect queues without booking
- Duomo (Santa Maria del Fiore): Cathedral FREE; Dome climb €30 (includes Baptistery, Campanile); book well in advance
- Piazzale Michelangelo: FREE (panoramic view of city, sunset ideal)
- Boboli Gardens: €10
- Bargello Museum: €8
- Palazzo Pitti: €16 (includes Boboli Gardens)
- Ponte Vecchio: FREE to walk across (iconic bridge with goldsmiths' shops)
- San Miniato al Monte: FREE (beautiful hilltop church)

VENICE (Venezia)
Built on 118 small islands connected by canals and bridges, Venice is unlike anywhere else in the world. It is one of the most expensive Italian cities.
- Population: ~250,000 (declining)
- Best for: Romance, architecture, art, unique experience
- Airport: Marco Polo (VCE) — water taxi to city €80–100 (shared €15); ATVO bus to Piazzale Roma €10
- Getting around: Vaporetto (water bus) — single ticket €9.50; 24h pass €25; 48h pass €35; 72h pass €45

Key attractions in Venice:
- St. Mark's Basilica: FREE basic entry; no shorts or bare shoulders; Pala d'Oro (golden altar) €5; Treasury €5; Museum €7
- Doge's Palace: €25 (includes Bridge of Sighs view)
- Rialto Bridge and Market: FREE
- Peggy Guggenheim Collection: €18
- Gallerie dell'Accademia: €15
- Murano Island (glassblowing): FREE to visit; factory demos sometimes free
- Burano Island (colorful houses): FREE; €15–20 return vaporetto from Venice
- Campanile di San Marco: €10 (great views)

Note: Venice charges a day-tripper fee of €5 on peak days (2024 pilot scheme may expand)

MILAN (Milano)
Italy's fashion and finance capital, Milan is modern, cosmopolitan and expensive.
- Population: ~1.4 million
- Best for: Fashion, design, business, opera, Leonardo da Vinci's Last Supper
- Airport: Malpensa (MXP) — main hub; Linate (LIN) — city airport; Bergamo (BGY) — Ryanair
- Getting around: Metro (M1–M5), trams, buses

Key attractions in Milan:
- The Last Supper (Cenacolo): €15 + €2 booking fee (MANDATORY advance booking; 15-minute visits; book 2+ months ahead)
- Duomo di Milano: Cathedral €5; Rooftop terraces €13 (stairs) or €17 (lift); Museum €5
- Pinacoteca di Brera: €15
- Pinacoteca Ambrosiana: €20
- Castello Sforzesco: €5 (museums inside)
- Navigli District: FREE to wander (canal area, great for aperitivo)
- Galleria Vittorio Emanuele II: FREE (luxury shopping arcade, photo stop)

NAPLES (Napoli)
Chaotic, vibrant, and home to the world's best pizza, Naples is the gateway to Pompeii, the Amalfi Coast, and Capri.
- Population: ~900,000
- Best for: Authentic Italian culture, pizza, Pompeii day trip, Amalfi Coast
- Airport: Capodichino (NAP)
- Getting around: Metro, buses (often chaotic); Circumvesuviana train to Pompeii

Key attractions in Naples and surroundings:
- Naples National Archaeological Museum: €20 (world's best Roman artifacts)
- Pompeii Archaeological Park: €16 (day trip; ~30 min by Circumvesuviana from Naples Centrale, €2.90 one-way)
- Herculaneum (Ercolano): €13 (smaller, better preserved than Pompeii; 20 min from Naples)
- Mount Vesuvius: €12 entry to park + guided walk; organized tours from Naples €25–40
- Amalfi Coast day trip: organized tour €40–70; rent scooter €30–50/day; ferry Sorrento–Positano €20
- Capri island day trip: ferry from Naples €25–30 return; funicular up to town €2.50 each way
- Caserta Royal Palace: €14 (Versailles of Italy, 40 min from Naples)

TRANSPORTATION
--------------

Getting to Italy:
- Flights from Athens to Rome/Milan: €50–180 return (Ryanair, Wizz Air, Aegean)
- Flights from Northern Europe: €80–250 return

Within Italy by train (Trenitalia / Italo):
- Rome to Florence: ~1.5h, €20–50 (Frecciarossa high-speed); budget trains €10–15 (2–3h)
- Florence to Venice: ~2h, €20–45 (high-speed)
- Rome to Naples: ~1.2h, €20–40 (Frecciarossa); slower trains €12 (2.5h)
- Rome to Milan: ~3h, €30–70 (high-speed)
- Milan to Venice: ~2.5h, €25–55

Budget tip: Book trains 60–90 days in advance for best prices. Frecciarossa trains have cheaper "Super Economy" fares.

Local transport per day:
- Rome metro/bus daily pass: €7
- Venice vaporetto 24h: €25
- Florence: Mostly walkable; day bus pass €5
- Milan: 24h metro pass: €7

FOOD AND DINING
---------------

Italy has a strong café culture. Budget travelers can eat very well cheaply.

Typical costs:
- Espresso at bar (standing): €1–1.50
- Cornetto (croissant): €1–1.50
- Pizza (by the slice, al taglio): €2–4 per slice
- Whole pizza at pizzeria: €8–15
- Pasta dish at trattoria: €10–18
- Full meal (primo + secondo + water + wine): €20–35
- Gelato (2 scoops): €2.50–4
- Aperitivo (Aperol Spritz): €5–10
- Supermarket sandwich/panini: €3–5
- Street food (supplì, arancini): €1.50–3

BUDGET PLANNING
---------------

3-DAY ITINERARY — ROME (Budget: €350–450 total / Mid: €550–700 / Luxury: €900+)

Day 1 — Ancient Rome:
- Colosseum + Roman Forum + Palatine Hill: €18–24
- Lunch near Testaccio: pizza al taglio €5–8
- Capitoline Museums: €15
- Evening at Trastevere (dinner): €15–25
Daily total budget: ~€70–100 + accommodation

Day 2 — Vatican & Piazzas:
- Vatican Museums + Sistine Chapel: €20
- St. Peter's (free) + Dome (€8–10)
- Lunch: sandwich near Prati €5–8
- Piazza Navona + Pantheon (€5) + Trevi Fountain
- Dinner in Monti: €20–30
Daily total: ~€65–90 + accommodation

Day 3 — Borghese & Shopping:
- Borghese Gallery: €15 (must book in advance)
- Spanish Steps + Via Condotti
- Lunch: suppli €3 + beer €4
- Sunset at Pincio Terrace (free)
- Final dinner in Trastevere: €25–35
Daily total: ~€60–85 + accommodation

3-day Rome budget summary (excluding flights):
- Budget: Hostel €25–35/night × 3 = €75–105 + activities/food €180–240 = TOTAL ~€255–345
- Mid-range: Hotel €130–160/night × 3 = €390–480 + activities/food €250–330 = TOTAL ~€640–810
- Luxury: Hotel €250–400/night × 3 = €750–1,200 + activities/food €400+ = TOTAL ~€1,150–1,600+

5-DAY ITINERARY — ROME + FLORENCE (Budget ~€500–650 / Mid ~€900–1,200)

Day 1–2: Rome (see above, 2 days)
Day 3: Travel Rome→Florence (2h train €20–40) + arrive Florence, afternoon Ponte Vecchio + Oltrarno walk + dinner
Day 4: Florence — Uffizi Gallery (€25) + Accademia/David (€16) + lunch at Mercato Centrale (€8–12) + Duomo area
Day 5: Florence — Piazzale Michelangelo morning (free) + Boboli Gardens (€10) + Tuscan lunch + depart

5-day budget summary:
- Budget: ~€500–650 (hostels, budget trains, self-catering some meals)
- Mid-range: ~€900–1,200 (3-star hotels, restaurant lunches and dinners, Frecciarossa trains)
- Luxury: ~€1,800–2,500+ (4-star hotels, fine dining, private tours)

7-DAY ITINERARY — ROME + FLORENCE + VENICE (Budget ~€750–950 / Mid ~€1,400–1,800)

Day 1–2: Rome
Day 3: Florence (train ~€20–40)
Day 4: Florence
Day 5: Venice (train from Florence ~2h, €20–45)
Day 6: Venice — islands (Murano, Burano) + sunset at Rialto
Day 7: Venice — Doge's Palace (€25) + San Marco area + depart

7-day budget summary:
- Budget: €750–950 (hostels €25–35/night, budget trains, self-catering, Mestre option for Venice)
- Mid-range: €1,400–1,800 (3-star hotels, most meals out, Frecciarossa trains)
- Luxury: €2,800–4,500+ (luxury hotels including Venice 5-star)

MONEY SAVING TIPS FOR ITALY
-----------------------------
1. Book trains 60–90 days ahead for super economy fares
2. Book museums online to avoid queues (and sometimes save on ticket)
3. The "aperitivo" tradition in Milan/Bologna: pay €8–12 for a drink and get free buffet food
4. Museum freebies: first Sunday of each month, state museums are FREE (huge queues though)
5. Eat lunch standing at a bar — same food, 30–50% cheaper than seated
6. Avoid tourist traps around major sights (Colosseum area restaurants charge 3×)
7. Buy a refillable water bottle — "nasoni" (drinking fountains) are FREE everywhere in Rome
8. Stay in Mestre or Padua to save on Venice accommodation
9. Use Trenitalia's "Carnet" options if taking multiple regional trains
10. Combo ticket Rome: Colosseum + Forum + Palatine is better value than separate

BEST TIME TO VISIT ITALY
-------------------------
- Spring (April–June): BEST — warm, not too crowded, all sights open
- Summer (July–August): Hot (35°C+), very crowded, most expensive, long queues
- Autumn (Sept–Oct): Excellent — still warm, fewer crowds, harvest season
- Winter (Nov–March): Cheapest, few tourists, cold in north; Rome pleasant at 12–15°C
