# Hiring Process

Atlas Systems' hiring process is designed to be efficient and respectful of candidates' time. The full process typically takes 2 to 3 weeks from application to offer.

## How to Apply
Open positions are listed at **https://atlas.com/careers**. Candidates submit a CV and an optional short cover letter. All applications are reviewed by the Human Resources department within 5 business days.

## Process Steps

### 1. CV Screen (HR)
A recruiter from the Human Resources department reviews the application and confirms basic fit (location, role level, work authorisation).

### 2. Hiring Manager Call (30 minutes)
A short conversation with the hiring manager (the future direct manager of the role) to discuss background, motivation, and the role itself.

### 3. Technical Assessment (role-dependent)
- **Engineering / DevOps / Data & AI roles**: a take-home technical exercise (2-4 hours of candidate time) followed by a 60-minute review interview.
- **Non-technical roles**: a structured interview focused on role-specific competencies.

### 4. Team Interview (60–90 minutes)
A panel interview with 2-3 team members the candidate would work with. Focus: technical depth, collaboration style, communication.

### 5. Final Interview with Director
A 30-45 minute conversation with the relevant department director. Focus: long-term alignment, values, open questions on both sides.

### 6. Offer
Successful candidates receive an offer letter within 3 business days of the final interview. The offer is verbal first, then formal in writing.

## What We Do Not Do
- Whiteboard coding interviews (we use the take-home exercise instead).
- Trick questions or stress interviews.
- Unpaid trial work periods.

## Internships
Atlas Systems runs a summer internship programme for university students (8 weeks, paid). Applications open each February. Internships often convert to full-time offers.

## Diversity and Equal Opportunity
Atlas Systems is an equal-opportunity employer and recruits without regard to gender, ethnicity, age, disability, sexual orientation, or religious belief. The Code of Conduct applies from the first interaction onward.
