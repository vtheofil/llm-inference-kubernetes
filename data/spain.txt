SPAIN TRAVEL GUIDE
==================

Spain is one of Europe's most vibrant destinations, combining world-class art and architecture with lively nightlife, stunning beaches, and exceptional food. It offers great value compared to France or the UK.

MAIN CITIES
-----------

BARCELONA
Catalonia's capital and Spain's most visited city, Barcelona blends Gaudí's surreal architecture with a beach lifestyle and dynamic culture.
- Population: ~1.6 million
- Best for: Architecture (Gaudí), beaches, food, nightlife, art
- Airport: El Prat (BCN) — 15 km from center; Aerobus €6.75 one-way (35 min) or metro L9 Sud €5.50
- Getting around: Metro (T-Casual 10-trip card €12.15), bus, bike, walking

Key attractions in Barcelona:
- Sagrada Família: €26 (basic) / €36 (with towers access); MANDATORY advance booking; Gaudí's unfinished masterpiece
- Park Güell: €10 for Monumental Zone (advance booking required); surrounding park FREE
- Casa Batlló: €35–49 (magic nights €52); stunning Gaudí façade
- Casa Milà (La Pedrera): €28 (rooftop + museum); €38 with evening experience
- Palau de la Música Catalana: €20 (guided tour); UNESCO World Heritage
- Picasso Museum: €14 (€19 with temporary exhibition); closed Mondays; free first Sunday
- MACBA (contemporary art): €12; free Thursday 16:00–20:00
- Gothic Quarter (Barri Gòtic): FREE to wander
- La Boqueria Market: FREE entry (tourist trap now but still beautiful)
- Barceloneta Beach: FREE
- Montjuïc Castle: €9 (panoramic views over city and port)
- Camp Nou stadium tour: €30 (FC Barcelona)
- Fundació Joan Miró: €15

MADRID
Spain's capital, Madrid is a walkable, cosmopolitan city with world-class museums, royal palaces, and a legendary nightlife scene.
- Population: ~3.3 million
- Best for: Art (Prado, Reina Sofía, Thyssen), food (tapas), Royal Palace, nightlife
- Airport: Adolfo Suárez Barajas (MAD); Metro Line 8: €3 + €3 airport supplement = €6; taxi: €32 flat rate
- Getting around: Metro (10-trip card: €12.20; airport supplement extra), bus, walking

Key attractions in Madrid:
- Museo Nacional del Prado: €15 (one of world's greatest art museums; Velázquez, Goya, El Greco); free Mon–Sat 18:00–20:00 and Sun 17:00–20:00
- Museo Reina Sofía: €12 (Picasso's Guernica); free Mon, Wed–Sat 19:00–21:00, Sun 13:30–21:00
- Museo Thyssen-Bornemisza: €14 (permanent); free Mondays
- Palacio Real (Royal Palace): €14 (€10 without gardens); largest royal palace in Western Europe
- Parque del Retiro: FREE (beautiful park; rowboats €6 for 45 min)
- Puerta del Sol: FREE (city center, 0km marker for Spain)
- Plaza Mayor: FREE (historic square, cafés around it are touristy and pricey)
- Templo de Debod: FREE (Egyptian temple, sunset views)
- El Rastro flea market: FREE (Sundays 09:00–15:00; huge outdoor market)
- Mercado de San Miguel: FREE entry (gourmet market, eat tapas inside €2–5 each)

SEVILLE (Sevilla)
Capital of Andalusia, Seville is the most romantic Spanish city — home to flamenco, tapas, and stunning Moorish architecture.
- Population: ~690,000
- Best for: Flamenco, Moorish architecture, tapas culture, holy week (Easter), FC Sevilla
- Getting around: Tram, bus, cycling, walking
- Airports: San Pablo (SVQ)

Key attractions in Seville:
- Real Alcázar: €14.50 (magnificent Moorish-Renaissance palace; UNESCO); book online; evening visits €19.50
- Catedral de Sevilla + Giralda Tower: €12 (world's largest Gothic cathedral; Christopher Columbus's tomb); free Monday mornings
- Plaza de España: FREE (stunning 1929 exhibition pavilion; ideal morning visit)
- Metropol Parasol (Las Setas): €5 including €5 drink voucher (panoramic rooftop; go at sunset)
- Flamenco show: €20–30 (tablao shows at various venues; 1 hour)
- Torre del Oro (Gold Tower): €3
- Barrio Santa Cruz: FREE (old Jewish quarter, whitewashed streets)

GRANADA
Home to the Alhambra — arguably the greatest Islamic palace in the world.
- Population: ~230,000
- Best for: Alhambra, tapas (free with drinks!), Sacromonte caves, Sierra Nevada

Key attractions in Granada:
- Alhambra Palace + Generalife Gardens + Alcazaba: €19–14 depending on ticket type; BOOK 90+ DAYS IN ADVANCE (sells out far ahead)
- Cathedral of Granada: €5
- Royal Chapel (Capilla Real): €5 (Ferdinand and Isabella buried here)
- Sacromonte neighborhood: FREE (caves carved into hillside; cave flamenco shows €20–30)
- Albaicín neighborhood: FREE (UNESCO Moorish quarter; views of Alhambra from San Nicolás viewpoint)
- Tapas culture: In Granada, tapas are FREE with every drink ordered (tradition unique to Granada)! A beer costs €2.50–3.50 and includes a free tapa.

TRANSPORTATION
--------------

Getting to Spain:
- Flights Athens to Barcelona/Madrid: €60–180 return (Ryanair, Vueling, Aegean, Iberia)
- Budget airlines: Ryanair often Athens→Madrid or Athens→Barcelona from €40 one-way

Within Spain by train (Renfe):
- Madrid to Barcelona: ~2.5h AVE high-speed, €30–80 (book 60+ days for promos)
- Madrid to Seville: ~2.5h AVE, €25–65
- Madrid to Granada: ~3h AVE (no direct; change in Antequera), €40–70; or 4.5h bus €15–25
- Barcelona to Valencia: ~3h by train, €20–50; or cheap bus €15–25
- Seville to Granada: no train; bus 3h €15–22 (ALSA)

BlaBlaCar is popular in Spain: Madrid–Barcelona ~€15–25, Madrid–Seville ~€12–20

Bus network:
- ALSA buses: comprehensive, cheap. Madrid–Seville €20–35 (2.5h AVE vs 6h bus — train wins on this route)
- Flixbus: Madrid–Barcelona from €6 (9h overnight)

FOOD AND DINING
---------------

Spain has one of the world's great food cultures. Eating out is affordable.

Typical costs:
- Coffee (café con leche): €1.50–2.50
- Churros con chocolate: €3–5
- Bocadillo (sandwich): €3–5
- Menú del día (3-course lunch with wine and bread): €10–15 (EXCELLENT VALUE; restaurant quality food at café prices)
- Tapas: €2–4 each (in most of Spain; FREE with drinks in Granada)
- Pintxos (Basque tapas): €2–3.50 each
- Pizza/pasta: €9–14 for main
- Full dinner at restaurant: €20–35
- Glass of wine: €2–5
- Beer (caña, small glass): €1.50–3
- Sangria (jug): €10–18

Food tips:
- Menú del día is THE best deal in Spain — 3 courses + wine + bread for €10–14 at any sit-down restaurant
- Mercado de la Boqueria (Barcelona) and Mercado de San Miguel (Madrid) for cheap quality food
- In Granada: order drinks, get free tapas — budget travelers can eat for almost nothing
- Supermarket Mercadona is cheap and excellent for self-catering

BUDGET PLANNING
---------------

3-DAY BARCELONA ITINERARY

Budget approach (~€350–450 / Mid €600–800 / Luxury €1,200+)

Day 1 — Gaudí:
- Sagrada Família (€26–36): book online; morning arrival
- Lunch: local restaurant near Eixample, Menú del día €12
- Park Güell (€10): pre-booked; surrounding park free
- Evening: Gracia neighborhood tapas crawl €15–20

Day 2 — Old Town and Beach:
- Gothic Quarter walk: FREE
- La Boqueria: FREE entry; eat inside €5–10
- Picasso Museum (€14)
- Barceloneta beach: FREE; afternoon swim
- Dinner: El Born neighborhood restaurants €15–25

Day 3 — More Gaudí / Art:
- Casa Milà (€28) or Casa Batlló (€35)
- Passeig de Gràcia photo walk: FREE
- MACBA if interested in contemporary art (€12)
- Montjuïc cable car (€12.70) + castle (€9) for views
- Final tapas dinner in Poble Sec: €20–30

3-day Barcelona budget breakdown:
- Budget: Hostel €30–40/night × 3 = €90–120 + Metro card €12 + Activities €60–80 + Food €100–130 = TOTAL ~€265–345
- Mid-range: Hotel €150/night × 3 = €450 + Metro €25 + Activities €120 + Food €180 = TOTAL ~€775
- Luxury: Hotel €280/night × 3 = €840 + Activities €180 + Food €280 = TOTAL ~€1,300+

5-DAY ITINERARY — BARCELONA + MADRID

Day 1–2: Barcelona (see above, 2 days)
Day 3: AVE train Barcelona→Madrid (2.5h, €35–60); afternoon Prado Museum (free 18:00–20:00)
Day 4: Madrid — Reina Sofía (Guernica) + Royal Palace + Retiro Park (FREE) + tapas evening in Malasaña
Day 5: Madrid — Thyssen Museum + El Rastro Sunday market (if Sunday) + Templo de Debod at sunset

5-day budget summary:
- Budget: ~€450–600 (hostels, Flixbus or budget AVE tickets, free museum hours, tapas)
- Mid-range: ~€850–1,100
- Luxury: ~€1,800–2,800+

7-DAY ITINERARY — MADRID + ANDALUSIA

Day 1–2: Madrid (museums, Royal Palace, Retiro)
Day 3: AVE Madrid→Seville (2.5h, €35–60); afternoon Barrio Santa Cruz + Plaza de España
Day 4: Seville — Alcázar (€14.50) + Cathedral + Giralda (€12) + flamenco show (€25)
Day 5: Bus Seville→Granada (3h, €18); arrive afternoon; free tapas evening
Day 6: Granada — Alhambra (€19; MUST book 90+ days ahead) + Albaicín evening
Day 7: Granada — Sacromonte + Cathedral + depart or continue to Málaga/coast

7-day budget summary:
- Budget: ~€650–850 (mix hostels/cheap hotels, buses, free tapas in Granada)
- Mid-range: ~€1,200–1,600
- Luxury: ~€2,500–4,000+

MONEY SAVING TIPS FOR SPAIN
-----------------------------
1. Alhambra tickets sell out months ahead — book the moment you decide to go to Granada
2. Prado, Reina Sofía, Thyssen all have free evening hours — plan your visit then
3. Menú del día is mandatory: best food, lowest prices, typically €10–14
4. Granada tapas culture: your entire food bill could be €10/day if you stick to bars with drink rounds
5. AVE trains have cheap "Básico" fares (no changes/refunds) from €20–30 if booked 60+ days ahead
6. Barcelona: buy T-Casual 10-trip metro card (€12.15) vs €2.55 per single journey
7. MACBA and many city museums free on Sunday evenings
8. BlaBlaCar for intercity travel: Madrid–Seville €12–20 vs €25–65 by AVE
9. Airbnb and Hostelworld: Spain has excellent hostel scene with private rooms for €30–50
10. Tapas bars around Plaza Mayor and Ramblas = tourist trap; go 2 streets away for local prices

BEST TIME TO VISIT SPAIN
-------------------------
- Spring (March–May): BEST — pleasant temperatures, Semana Santa (Holy Week) in Seville (spectacular but crowded)
- Summer (June–Aug): Hot (35–40°C in Seville/Granada), beaches perfect; Seville is uncomfortably hot in July–August
- Autumn (Sept–Oct): Excellent — still warm, fewer tourists, grape harvest in Rioja
- Winter (Nov–Feb): Mild in south; Madrid cold; Barcelona pleasant (15°C); cheapest prices; ski season in Pyrenees/Sierra Nevada
