GREECE TRAVEL GUIDE
===================

Greece is one of the world's most iconic destinations, with a history spanning thousands of years, 6,000 islands and islets, a warm Mediterranean climate, and some of the best food and hospitality on the planet. It offers excellent value for money.

MAIN CITIES AND DESTINATIONS
-----------------------------

ATHENS (Αθήνα)
Greece's capital and one of the world's oldest cities, Athens is a vibrant metropolis surrounding the ancient Acropolis.
- Population: ~4 million (metro)
- Best for: Ancient history, culture, food, nightlife, island ferry connections
- Airport: Eleftherios Venizelos (ATH) — Metro Line 3: €10 one-way (40 min); Express bus X95 to Syntagma: €6 (70 min); taxi: €40–55 flat rate (day/night)
- Getting around: Metro (3 lines), trams, trolley buses, taxis, walking

Key attractions in Athens:
- Acropolis + Parthenon: €20 (April–Oct) / €10 (Nov–March); combined ticket €30 (includes 6 ancient sites for 5 days): BEST VALUE
- Acropolis Museum: €15 (world-class modern museum); first Sunday Nov–March: FREE; reduced €8 Sunday mornings (Oct–March)
- Ancient Agora + Stoa of Attalos museum: included in €30 combined ticket
- Temple of Olympian Zeus: included in €30 combined ticket
- Hadrian's Arch: FREE (visible from outside)
- Kerameikos archaeological site: included in combined ticket
- Lycabettus Hill: FREE walk (308m); cable car €7 round trip; best panoramic view of Athens
- National Archaeological Museum: €12 (world's best collection of ancient Greek artifacts); free first Sunday of month (Nov–April)
- Benaki Museum (Greek history): €9; free Thursday evenings
- Byzantine and Christian Museum: €8
- Plaka neighborhood: FREE to walk (picturesque streets below Acropolis)
- Monastiraki Flea Market: FREE (Sunday morning best); antiques, vintage, souvenirs
- Syntagma Square + Parliament: FREE (Evzone changing of guard daily)
- Panathenaic Stadium (Kallimarmaro): €10 (world's only marble stadium; hosted 1896 Olympics)

Athens combined ticket (€30): Covers Acropolis, Ancient Agora, Kerameikos, Temple of Olympian Zeus, Roman Agora, Hadrian's Library. Valid for 5 days. EXCELLENT VALUE.

MYKONOS
The island of glamour, beaches, and world-class nightlife on the Cyclades.
- Population: ~10,000 (swells to 100,000+ in summer)
- Getting there: Ferry from Piraeus (Athens): 4.5h (conventional) or 2.5–3h (high-speed); Hellenic Seaways/Sea Jets/Blue Star Ferries; tickets €30–65 each way; or fly from Athens: 40 min, €40–120
- Getting around: Local buses €2–3 per ride; taxis (scarce and expensive €20–50 per ride); ATV/scooter rental €25–40/day; water taxis
- Best beaches: Paradise Beach, Super Paradise, Elia (free to access; sunbeds €20–40/day in summer)
- Mykonos Town (Chora): windmills, Little Venice, narrow streets
- Nightlife: world-famous; clubs open from midnight; drinks €15–25 each
- NOTE: Mykonos is EXPENSIVE. Budget travelers should avoid July–August.

SANTORINI
Famous for its caldera views, blue-domed churches, and dramatic sunsets — one of the world's most photographed destinations.
- Population: ~15,000 (summer peak 50,000+)
- Getting there: Ferry from Piraeus: 8h (conventional) or 5h (high-speed); tickets €35–75; or fly from Athens 45 min, €40–120
- Getting around: Local buses €2–3; ATV/scooter rental €25–40/day; taxi €20–60 per ride
- Key spots: Oia (sunset) — FREE; Fira (capital); Imerovigli; Akrotiri archaeological site: €12; Wine Museum: €5–10
- Caldera view villages: Oia (most crowded), Imerovigli (quieter, same views)
- Black sand beaches: Kamari, Perissa; free access; sunbeds €8–15/pair
- Sunset at Oia: arrive 2+ hours early in summer to claim a spot

THESSALONIKI
Greece's second city — culturally rich, with great food, Byzantine heritage, and a more local, authentic feel than Athens.
- Population: ~810,000
- Best for: Byzantine monuments, food (souvlaki scene, bougatsa, fresh fish), nightlife, Macedonian heritage
- Getting to/from Athens: Train 4h €20–30; bus 6h €20–25; fly 45 min €30–80
- Key sites: White Tower (€4); Archaeological Museum (€10); Arch of Galerius (FREE); Rotunda (FREE); Upper Town Ano Poli (FREE; Byzantine walls and views); Seafront Promenade (FREE)
- Aristotelous Square: FREE (grand central square; café culture)

CRETE (Κρήτη)
The largest Greek island, with a distinct culture, history, and stunning landscapes.
- Getting there: Ferry from Piraeus to Heraklion: 9h overnight; €25–55; ferry from Piraeus to Chania: 9h overnight; or fly from Athens: 50 min, €30–100
- Main towns: Heraklion (capital), Chania (most beautiful), Rethymno (Venetian), Agios Nikolaos
- Knossos Palace (Minoan): €15 (outside Heraklion); can hire guide €10–15 extra
- Samaria Gorge hike: €5 entry; 16km one-way hike; legendary; boat from Agia Roumeli back €15
- Heraklion Archaeological Museum: €12 (world's best Minoan collection)
- Chania Old Town (Venetian harbor): FREE to walk; beautiful lighthouse; best sunset spot
- Beaches: Elafonisi (lagoon/pink sand), Balos Lagoon, Falassarna — all accessible; boat tours to Balos €25

TRANSPORTATION
--------------

Domestic ferries (from Piraeus port, Athens):
- Piraeus to Santorini: ~€40 conventional (8h) / ~€55–75 high-speed (5h)
- Piraeus to Mykonos: ~€35–45 conventional (4.5h) / ~€50–65 high-speed (2.5h)
- Piraeus to Crete (Heraklion): ~€30–50 conventional (9h, overnight)
- Book via Ferryscanner, Ferries.gr, or directly with Blue Star / Sea Jets / Hellenic Seaways
- TIP: Overnight ferries to Crete save a night's accommodation (cabin berths €15–25 extra)

Domestic flights (Athens to islands):
- Athens to Santorini: ~40 min, €40–100 (Aegean/Olympic/Sky Express)
- Athens to Mykonos: ~40 min, €40–100
- Athens to Heraklion (Crete): ~50 min, €30–90
- Athens to Rhodes: ~1h, €40–120

Inter-island ferries:
- Mykonos to Santorini: ~2.5h high-speed, €45–65
- Santorini to Crete: ~2h high-speed, €40–60

Athens metro:
- Single ticket: €1.40 (90 min)
- 24h ticket: €4.10
- 5-day tourist ticket: €9 (excellent value)
- 3-journey card: €4

FOOD AND DINING
---------------

Greek food is exceptional and affordable. Dining out is a pleasure and a bargain compared to Western Europe.

Typical costs:
- Greek coffee (freddo espresso): €2–3.50
- Bougatsa (pastry): €2–3
- Souvlaki/pita: €2.50–4 (street food, best cheap meal in Greece)
- Gyros pita: €2.50–3.50
- Spanakopita (spinach pie) from bakery: €2–3
- Mezze plate (hummus, tzatziki, olives, bread): €5–10 at restaurant
- Taverna main course (moussaka, pastitsio, grilled fish): €10–18
- Full taverna meal (starters + main + dessert + wine/beer): €18–30/person
- Fresh fish at taverna: €15–35 per kg (sold by weight; ask for price first)
- Glass of ouzo: €2–4
- Greek beer (Mythos, Fix): €3–5 at bar
- House wine (carafe, 500ml): €5–10

Food tips:
- Souvlaki is the Greek fast food — excellent quality for €2.50–4
- Tavernas in tourist areas charge 50–100% more; go one street away
- "Mezze" culture: order many small plates to share, excellent for trying variety
- Fresh fish: ask price per kg BEFORE ordering (can be expensive; 300–400g typical portion)
- Greek yogurt with honey at any café: €3–5 (much better than supermarket)

BUDGET PLANNING
---------------

3-DAY ATHENS ITINERARY

Budget approach (~€250–350 / Mid €450–600 / Luxury €900+)

Day 1 — Ancient Athens:
- Acropolis + Parthenon (€20 or €30 combo): go early morning (opens 08:00) to avoid crowds and heat
- Acropolis Museum (€15): excellent modern museum
- Lunch: souvlaki in Monastiraki €3–4
- Ancient Agora (included in combo): walk through history
- Evening: Plaka neighborhood walk (FREE) + ouzo at rooftop bar
- Dinner: Taverna in Psiri €15–20

Day 2 — Culture and Neighborhoods:
- National Archaeological Museum (€12): worth 2–3 hours
- Omonia and Exarcheia neighborhoods (FREE, local Athens experience)
- Lunch: Menú set lunch or pastry shop €6–10
- Monastiraki flea market (FREE) + Kerameikos (combo)
- Sunset from Lycabettus Hill (FREE walk or €7 cable car)
- Dinner: seafood in Mikrolimano (Piraeus, 20 min metro) or Glyfada

Day 3 — Day Trip or Beaches:
Option A: Cape Sounion + Temple of Poseidon bus tour: €20–25 organized tour or KTEL bus €8 each way, site entry €10
Option B: Day trip to Hydra island: ferry ~2h from Piraeus, €30 return; car-free island
Option C: Athens Riviera beaches (Glyfada, Vouliagmeni): tram + bus €3; Vouliagmeni Lake entry €15

3-day Athens budget summary:
- Budget: Hostel €25–35/night × 3 = €75–105 + Metro €10 + Activities €50–65 + Food €90–120 = TOTAL ~€225–300
- Mid-range: Hotel €110/night × 3 = €330 + Activities €80 + Food €150 = TOTAL ~€560
- Luxury: Hotel €250/night × 3 = €750 + Activities €100 + Food €250 = TOTAL ~€1,100+

5-DAY ITINERARY — ATHENS + SANTORINI

Day 1–2: Athens (Acropolis, museums, neighborhoods)
Day 3: Ferry or fly to Santorini (fly €50–80, 45 min; or fast ferry €55–75, 5h); arrive afternoon; sunset in Oia
Day 4: Santorini — Akrotiri site (€12) + Fira walk + caldera hike Fira–Oia (9km, 3–4h, stunning views, FREE)
Day 5: Santorini — beaches (Perissa/Kamari) + wine tasting at winery €15–25 + evening in Oia

5-day budget summary:
- Budget: ~€450–600 (hostel Athens, budget accommodation Santorini beach side, fast ferry)
- Mid-range: ~€900–1,200 (3-star hotels including Santorini mid-range, flights both directions)
- Luxury: ~€2,000–5,000+ (caldera infinity pool hotels, fine dining)

7-DAY ITINERARY — ATHENS + SANTORINI + MYKONOS

Day 1–2: Athens
Day 3: Athens → Santorini (fly or ferry)
Day 4: Santorini exploration
Day 5: Santorini → Mykonos (fast ferry, ~2.5h, €45–65)
Day 6: Mykonos — beach day (Paradise or Elia) + Mykonos Town evening
Day 7: Mykonos → Athens by plane (45 min, €50–80) + depart

7-day budget summary:
- Budget: ~€700–950 (hostels, budget ferries, souvlaki diet, free beaches)
- Mid-range: ~€1,400–1,900 (3-star, some restaurant dinners, flights for speed)
- Luxury: ~€3,000–8,000+ (Santorini caldera suite, Mykonos beach club)

MONEY SAVING TIPS FOR GREECE
------------------------------
1. Athens combined site ticket (€30) is mandatory — saves €30+ vs individual entries
2. National museum and Acropolis museum free on first Sunday (November–April)
3. Souvlaki + pita = best €3 meal in Europe; eat it for lunch every day
4. Book ferry tickets 4–8 weeks ahead in summer — prices rise dramatically and some routes sell out
5. Overnight ferries to Crete = free accommodation night (€25–50 for deck, €50–70 for cabin)
6. Avoid Mykonos in July–August unless you have a large budget; shoulder season prices 50% less
7. Stay in Perissa/Kamari (Santorini) — same island, fraction of Oia/Fira caldera prices
8. Athens metro 5-day pass (€9) easily pays for itself in 2 days of sightseeing
9. Hydra island day trip from Athens: car-free, picturesque, €30 return ferry
10. Temple of Poseidon at Sounion: go by KTEL bus (€8 return) vs organized tour (€25)

BEST TIME TO VISIT GREECE
--------------------------
- Spring (April–May): BEST — warm, flowers blooming, no crowds, reasonable prices, Acropolis without queues
- Early summer (June): Excellent — summer heat starts, season opens on islands, not yet peak prices
- Summer (July–August): Peak — very hot (35–40°C), packed with tourists, most expensive
- Autumn (Sept–Oct): BEST for islands — sea still warm, crowds gone, prices drop 30–50%
- Winter (Nov–March): Athens and Thessaloniki fine; most islands shut down; skiing possible (Parnassos, Kaimaktsalan)
