=== ROME (ROMA) ===
Capital of Italy, one of Europe's most visited cities. Best visited year-round, avoid August (crowded, hot).

Average daily costs:
- Budget traveller: 60-80 EUR/day (hostel, street food, free sights)
- Mid-range: 120-180 EUR/day (3-star hotel, sit-down meals, main attractions)
- Luxury: 300+ EUR/day

Top attractions & entry fees:
- Colosseum + Roman Forum + Palatine Hill: 18 EUR (combined ticket, book online to skip queues)
- Vatican Museums + Sistine Chapel: 20 EUR (book in advance, 3-4h visit)
- St. Peter's Basilica: FREE (dome climb 8 EUR)
- Trevi Fountain: FREE
- Pantheon: 5 EUR
- Borghese Gallery: 15 EUR (must book in advance)
- Castel Sant'Angelo: 15 EUR

Getting around Rome:
- Metro ticket: 1.50 EUR
- 24h transit pass: 7 EUR
- 48h transit pass: 12.50 EUR
- Taxi from airport (Fiumicino FCO): ~50 EUR flat rate
- Train from FCO airport to city centre: 14 EUR (35 min)

Food costs:
- Pizza slice (al taglio): 2-4 EUR
- Cappuccino: 1.20-2 EUR
- Gelato: 2-4 EUR
- Sit-down trattoria meal (pasta + water): 12-20 EUR
- Mid-range restaurant (2 courses + wine): 30-50 EUR

Best neighbourhoods to stay:
- Trastevere: lively, local vibe, great nightlife
- Centro Storico: central to everything, pricier
- Prati: near Vatican, quieter, good value

Recommended stay: 3-4 days minimum

Sample 3-day Rome budget (mid-range, 2 people):
- Hotel (3 nights x 100 EUR): 300 EUR
- Food (3 days x 40 EUR/person): 240 EUR
- Attractions: ~80 EUR
- Transport: ~30 EUR
- TOTAL: ~650 EUR for 2 people (~325 EUR/person)

=== FLORENCE (FIRENZE) ===
Birthplace of the Renaissance. Compact and very walkable.

Average daily costs:
- Budget: 55-75 EUR/day
- Mid-range: 100-160 EUR/day

Top attractions:
- Uffizi Gallery: 20 EUR (book online, queue hours without booking)
- Accademia (Michelangelo's David): 16 EUR (book in advance)
- Duomo (Cathedral exterior): FREE | Cupola climb: 18 EUR | Full pass: 30 EUR
- Boboli Gardens: 10 EUR
- Palazzo Vecchio: 12.50 EUR

Food costs:
- Lampredotto sandwich (local street food): 5 EUR
- Ribollita soup (trattoria): 8-12 EUR
- Bistecca Fiorentina (T-bone, 2 portions): 50-80 EUR
- Gelato: 2-3 EUR

Recommended stay: 2-3 days

=== VENICE (VENEZIA) ===
Unique city built on water. Expensive but unmissable.

Average daily costs:
- Budget: 80-100 EUR/day
- Mid-range: 150-250 EUR/day

Top attractions:
- St. Mark's Basilica: FREE (treasury 3 EUR, museum 7 EUR)
- Doge's Palace: 20 EUR
- Gondola ride: 80-100 EUR per gondola (30 min), split between up to 6 people
- Rialto Bridge: FREE
- Murano island (glass blowing): FREE ferry + FREE to walk

Getting around:
- Vaporetto (water bus) single: 9.50 EUR
- 24h vaporetto pass: 25 EUR
- 48h pass: 35 EUR
- Walking is free and often faster

Tips: Stay on the mainland in Mestre to save money (30% cheaper hotels).
Recommended stay: 1-2 days

=== MILAN (MILANO) ===
Italy's fashion and finance capital. Modern, expensive.

Average daily costs:
- Budget: 70-90 EUR/day
- Mid-range: 130-200 EUR/day

Top attractions:
- Duomo di Milano: exterior FREE | interior 7 EUR | rooftop 14 EUR
- The Last Supper (da Vinci): 15 EUR — book 2-3 months in advance
- Sforza Castle: FREE (museums inside 5 EUR)
- Navigli canals: FREE

Recommended stay: 1-2 days

=== ITALY BUDGET PLANNING ===

Budget traveller (hostels, street food, free sights):
- Rome 4 days: 280-340 EUR
- Florence 2 days: 120-160 EUR
- Venice 2 days: 160-200 EUR
- Total 8 days Italy: 560-700 EUR + flights

Mid-range (3-star hotels, restaurants, all main sights):
- Rome 4 days: 500-700 EUR
- Florence 2 days: 220-340 EUR
- Venice 2 days: 300-500 EUR
- Total 8 days Italy: 1020-1540 EUR + flights

Flights to Italy from Greece:
- Athens to Rome: 40-120 EUR return (budget airlines: Ryanair, easyJet, Volotea)
- Athens to Venice: 50-130 EUR return
- Athens to Milan: 40-110 EUR return
- Book 6-8 weeks in advance for best prices

Best time to visit: April-June, September-October (avoid August crowds)
