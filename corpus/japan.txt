=== GENERAL JAPAN INFO ===
Currency: Japanese Yen (JPY). 1 EUR ≈ 160 JPY (approximate — always check current rate).
Language: Japanese. English is limited outside tourist areas — get Google Translate offline.
Safety: One of the safest countries in the world.
Best time to visit: March-April (cherry blossoms) | October-November (autumn leaves) — avoid July-August (humid, typhoon season).

IC Card (Suica/Pasmo): Rechargeable card for all trains, metros, and convenience stores. Buy at any major station.
Japan Rail Pass (JR Pass): For tourists, covers most bullet trains (shinkansen). Not always worth it for short trips.
- 7-day JR Pass: ~50,000 JPY (≈315 EUR)
- 14-day JR Pass: ~80,000 JPY (≈500 EUR)

=== TOKYO ===
Japan's capital. Massive, modern, efficient. Safe, clean, overwhelming in the best way.

Average daily costs:
- Budget: 60-80 EUR/day
- Mid-range: 120-180 EUR/day
- Luxury: 300+ EUR/day

Top attractions & entry fees:
- Senso-ji Temple (Asakusa): FREE
- Shibuya Crossing (most famous crossing in world): FREE
- Meiji Shrine: FREE
- Tokyo Skytree (observation deck): 2,100 JPY (≈13 EUR)
- Tokyo Tower: 1,200 JPY (≈7.50 EUR)
- teamLab Planets (digital art): 3,200 JPY (≈20 EUR) — book online
- Shinjuku Gyoen garden: 500 JPY (≈3 EUR)
- Harajuku (Takeshita Street): FREE
- Akihabara (electronics/anime district): FREE
- Tsukiji Outer Market: FREE

Getting around Tokyo:
- Metro/subway single: 170-320 JPY (≈1-2 EUR) depending on distance
- 24h metro pass: 600 JPY (≈3.75 EUR)
- IC card (Suica): Top up as you go
- Narita airport to city (N'EX train): 3,070 JPY (≈19 EUR)
- Haneda airport to city (Keikyu Line): 570 JPY (≈3.50 EUR)

Food costs (eating well is cheap in Japan):
- Ramen: 900-1,500 JPY (≈6-9 EUR)
- Sushi at conveyor belt (kaiten-zushi): 100-300 JPY/plate (≈0.60-2 EUR)
- Convenience store onigiri: 150-200 JPY (≈1 EUR)
- Bento box (conbini): 400-700 JPY (≈2.50-4.50 EUR)
- Tempura/tonkatsu set lunch: 900-1,500 JPY (≈6-9 EUR)
- High-end sushi omakase: 10,000-30,000 JPY (≈63-190 EUR)
- Matcha latte (café): 600-800 JPY (≈4-5 EUR)

Must-do Tokyo experiences:
- Day trip to Nikko (temples + nature): 2,500 JPY transport + 1,300 JPY entry
- Sumo tournament (if season): 4,000-15,000 JPY
- Robot Restaurant (Shinjuku): 8,000 JPY (touristy but fun)

Recommended stay: 4-5 days

=== KYOTO ===
Ancient capital, temples, geishas, bamboo forests. Heart of traditional Japan.

Average daily costs:
- Budget: 55-75 EUR/day
- Mid-range: 110-170 EUR/day

Top attractions:
- Fushimi Inari Shrine (thousands of torii gates): FREE (hike 2-3h)
- Arashiyama Bamboo Grove: FREE
- Kinkaku-ji (Golden Pavilion): 500 JPY (≈3 EUR)
- Nijo Castle: 800 JPY (≈5 EUR)
- Gion district (geisha area): FREE to walk
- Philosopher's Path: FREE
- Tenryu-ji Zen garden: 500 JPY (≈3 EUR)
- Nishiki Market: FREE

Shinkansen Tokyo → Kyoto: 13,600 JPY (≈85 EUR) — 2h15 on Nozomi/Hikari

Recommended stay: 3-4 days

=== OSAKA ===
Food capital of Japan. More affordable and casual than Tokyo or Kyoto.

Average daily costs:
- Budget: 50-70 EUR/day
- Mid-range: 100-150 EUR/day

Top attractions:
- Dotonbori (neon entertainment district): FREE
- Osaka Castle: 600 JPY (≈4 EUR)
- Shinsekai district: FREE
- Universal Studios Japan: 8,600 JPY (≈54 EUR) | with express pass: 12,000+ JPY

Food highlights (Osaka = eat yourself silly):
- Takoyaki (octopus balls): 500-800 JPY
- Okonomiyaki (savoury pancake): 800-1,500 JPY
- Kushikatsu (fried skewers): 150-300 JPY/skewer

Kyoto ↔ Osaka: 570 JPY local train (15 min) or shinkansen 1,500 JPY (13 min)

Recommended stay: 2-3 days

=== JAPAN BUDGET PLANNING ===

Tokyo 5 days (per person):
- Budget: 400-500 EUR
- Mid-range: 700-1,000 EUR

Tokyo 5 days + Kyoto 3 days + Osaka 2 days (10 days, per person):
- Budget (hostels, convenience store food): 800-1,100 EUR (excl. flights)
- Mid-range (business hotels, restaurants): 1,400-2,000 EUR (excl. flights)

Flights from Greece to Japan:
- Athens to Tokyo: 500-900 EUR return (with 1 layover)
- Best deals: Turkish Airlines via Istanbul, Emirates via Dubai, Qatar Airways via Doha
- Book 3-4 months in advance

Japan money tips:
- Japan is still largely cash-based — carry yen
- 7-Eleven, Lawson, FamilyMart ATMs accept foreign cards
- Budget airlines within Japan: Peach, Jetstar Japan (domestic)
- Most attractions significantly cheaper than Europe

Pocket WiFi rental: 3-5 EUR/day (pick up at airport) — essential!
